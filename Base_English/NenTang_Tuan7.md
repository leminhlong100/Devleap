# Tuần 7 — Reading & Listening Confidence (Tự tin Reading và Listening)

## 🎯 Mục tiêu & Trọng tâm tuần

- **Mục tiêu tuần:** Em đọc/nghe có chiến thuật, không hoảng khi không hiểu hết.
- **Trọng tâm ngữ pháp:** relative clauses, prepositions of time/place, articles review
- **Nhóm từ vựng:** environment, work, money, culture, media
- **Speaking:** talk about a place/object/person with details
- **Writing:** summary sentence and short response
- **Reading:** True/False/Not Given foundations, matching basics
- **Listening:** prediction, keywords, paraphrase lite
- **Nhiệm vụ nhẹ:** Tìm 10 biển hiệu hoặc đồ vật có chữ tiếng Anh quanh mình.
- **Mốc kiểm tra:** Trả lời được 10 câu hỏi Reading từ một đoạn ngắn.

## 📖 Ngữ pháp trọng tâm

### Articles

A/an dùng khi người nghe chưa biết chính xác cái nào hoặc khi nói một cái bất kỳ trong nhóm. The dùng khi người nghe biết cái nào hoặc khi đã nhắc trước đó. Người Việt hay bỏ articles vì tiếng Việt không có hệ thống tương đương rõ.

**Mẫu câu chính:**

| Mạo từ | Cách dùng |
| --- | --- |
| a/an | one general thing |
| the | specific/known thing |

**Câu đúng:**

- I bought a book yesterday. The book is about habits.
- She wants to become a doctor.
- The café near my school is quiet.
- I need an umbrella.

**Lỗi thường gặp của người học Việt Nam:**

| Câu sai | Câu đúng |
| --- | --- |
| I am student. | I am a student. |
| Sun is hot today. | The sun is hot today. |
| I bought book. | I bought a book. |

**Bài tập ngắn:** Chọn a/an/the hoặc không dùng mạo từ: I saw dog. dog was sleeping near school gate.

**Nguyên tắc sửa lỗi:** sửa một lỗi thật kỹ còn hơn làm 50 câu mà không biết mình sai vì sao.

### Prepositions — Học theo cảnh sử dụng

Prepositions are small but slippery. Learn them with scenes: at school, in the city, on the table, interested in, good at, depend on.

**Mẫu câu chính:** place/time/function chunks

**Câu đúng:**

- I am good at listening but weak in writing.
- She is interested in Korean music.
- The book is on the desk.
- I usually study at night.

**Lỗi thường gặp của người học Việt Nam:**

| Câu sai | Câu đúng |
| --- | --- |
| depend of | depend on |
| discuss about the problem | discuss the problem |
| married with | married to |

**Bài tập ngắn:** Vẽ bàn học và ghi nhãn 10 đồ vật bằng in/on/under/next to/near/between.

**Nguyên tắc sửa lỗi:** sửa một lỗi thật kỹ còn hơn làm 50 câu mà không biết mình sai vì sao.

### Relative Clauses

Mệnh đề quan hệ giúp người học diễn đạt rõ hơn mà không phải viết quá nhiều câu ngắn. Bắt đầu với who/which/that.

**Mẫu câu chính:** noun + who/which/that + detail

**Câu đúng:**

- I like teachers who explain things clearly.
- This is a book that helped me build habits.
- I prefer videos which have subtitles.
- Students who review regularly remember more.

**Lỗi thường gặp của người học Việt Nam:**

| Câu sai | Câu đúng |
| --- | --- |
| I like teacher explain clearly. | I like teachers who explain things clearly. |
| This is a place I usually study at there. | This is a place where I usually study. |
| The app which it is useful. | The app is useful. / The app which I use is useful. |

**Bài tập ngắn:** Mô tả 5 người và 5 đồ vật bằng who/which/that.

**Nguyên tắc sửa lỗi:** sửa một lỗi thật kỹ còn hơn làm 50 câu mà không biết mình sai vì sao.

## 🛠️ Kỹ năng & Khung mẫu — Reading

### 10.1 Vấn đề Reading sơ cấp

Nhiều bạn đọc IELTS như thế này:

1. Gặp từ lạ.
2. Hoảng.
3. Dịch từng chữ.
4. Quên câu trước.
5. Mất thời gian.
6. Chọn đại.

Mình cần một cách khác:

- **Before reading:** predict topic
- **First read:** get main idea
- **Second read:** find keywords and paraphrases
- **Answer:** choose based on evidence, not feeling
- **Review:** collect useful chunks

Người học chưa cần hiểu 100% văn bản mới có thể trả lời. Reading cần được xử lý bằng bằng chứng trong bài, không dựa vào cảm giác.

### 10.2 Bài đọc sơ cấp

Use these passages in three rounds:

1. Read for main idea.
2. Answer questions using evidence.
3. Collect 5 useful chunks.

#### Passage 1 — The Study Corner

A study corner does not need to be large or expensive. For many students, a small desk, a comfortable chair, and a quiet atmosphere are enough. What matters most is that the place helps the learner focus. Some students decorate their study corner with pictures, sticky notes, or a simple calendar. These small visual reminders can make learning feel more personal. However, a study corner should not be too crowded. If there are too many objects on the desk, students may get distracted instead of motivated.

**Questions**

1. What does a study corner not need to be?
2. What matters most about a study place?
3. Name two things students may use to decorate their study corner.
4. Why should a study corner not be too crowded?
5. What does 'these small visual reminders' refer to?

**Answer key**

1. Large or expensive.
2. It helps the learner focus.
3. Pictures, sticky notes, or a simple calendar.
4. Because students may get distracted.
5. Pictures, sticky notes, or a simple calendar.

#### Passage 2 — Learning Through Stories

Stories can make English easier to remember because they give words a situation. A word in a list is often lonely, but a word in a story has people, actions, places, and emotions around it. For example, the word 'nervous' becomes clearer when it appears in a story about a student before a speaking test. The learner can imagine the student's hands, voice, and thoughts. This connection helps the word become more meaningful. Teachers can use short stories, picture stories, or even personal stories to recycle vocabulary in a natural way.

**Questions**

1. Why can stories make English easier to remember?
2. What does the text mean by 'a word in a list is often lonely'?
3. Which example is used for the word 'nervous'?
4. What can teachers use to recycle vocabulary?
5. Is the passage mainly about grammar or vocabulary memory?

**Answer key**

1. Because they give words a situation.
2. The word has no context or connections.
3. A student before a speaking test.
4. Short stories, picture stories, or personal stories.
5. Vocabulary memory.

#### Passage 3 — Phones and Attention

Mobile phones are useful tools for language learners. Students can watch short videos, check pronunciation, save new words, and practise listening almost anywhere. However, phones can also damage attention. A learner may open an English video but then spend twenty minutes scrolling through unrelated posts. For this reason, some students use a simple rule: study first, scroll later. They put the phone on airplane mode, use only one learning app, and set a short timer. This does not remove all distractions, but it makes focused study more possible.

**Questions**

1. List two ways phones help language learners.
2. What problem can phones cause?
3. What simple rule do some students use?
4. Why do students put the phone on airplane mode?
5. Does the writer think phones are only bad?

**Answer key**

1. Watch videos, check pronunciation, save words, practise listening.
2. They can damage attention/cause distraction.
3. Study first, scroll later.
4. To reduce distractions and focus.
5. No, phones are useful but can distract learners.

#### Passage 4 — A Gentle Classroom

A gentle classroom is not an easy classroom. Students still need to practise, make effort, and accept correction. However, the atmosphere is different. In a gentle classroom, mistakes are treated as information, not shame. When a student says 'She go to school', the teacher does not laugh or simply say 'wrong'. Instead, the teacher may ask the class to notice the subject and repair the verb: 'She goes to school.' This small correction helps students understand the rule and feel safe enough to try again.

**Questions**

1. Is a gentle classroom the same as an easy classroom?
2. How are mistakes treated in a gentle classroom?
3. What incorrect sentence is given?
4. What is the corrected sentence?
5. Why is this kind of correction useful?

**Answer key**

1. No.
2. As information, not shame.
3. She go to school.
4. She goes to school.
5. It helps students understand the rule and feel safe to try again.

#### Passage 5 — The Power of Short Reviews

Many learners review only when they forget almost everything. This makes studying feel painful. A better method is to review in short sessions before knowledge disappears completely. For example, after learning ten useful phrases on Monday, a student can test them again on Tuesday, Thursday, and the following week. Each review does not need to be long. Even five minutes can help if the student tries to recall the phrases without looking first. The key is not reading the same notes again and again, but bringing the language back from memory.

**Questions**

1. When do many learners review?
2. What is a better method?
3. How long can a useful review be?
4. What should the student do before looking at notes?
5. What is the key idea of the passage?

**Answer key**

1. Only when they forget almost everything.
2. Review in short sessions before knowledge disappears completely.
3. Even five minutes.
4. Try to recall the phrases.
5. Short retrieval reviews help memory.

#### Passage 6 — Why Examples Matter

An idea becomes clearer when it has an example. In writing, many beginner students stop after a general sentence, such as 'Technology is useful for education.' This sentence is true, but it is not developed. A stronger paragraph gives a concrete example: students can watch science videos, join online classes, or use dictionaries on their phones. Examples help the reader see what the writer means. They also make the paragraph feel more real and less like a memorised answer.

**Questions**

1. What happens when an idea has an example?
2. What general sentence is mentioned?
3. Give two examples of technology in education from the text.
4. How do examples help the reader?
5. Why do examples make a paragraph better?

**Answer key**

1. It becomes clearer.
2. Technology is useful for education.
3. Watch science videos, join online classes, use dictionaries on phones.
4. They help the reader see what the writer means.
5. They make it more real and less memorised.

### 10.3 IELTS Reading Bridge — Question types

#### True, False, Not Given

- **True:** text says the same meaning.
- **False:** text says the opposite or contradicts.
- **Not Given:** text does not give enough information.

**Lưu ý cho người học sơ cấp:** Not Given không có nghĩa là "tôi không biết"; nó nghĩa là bài đọc chưa đưa đủ chứng cứ.

#### Matching headings

Câu hỏi cần đặt ra là đoạn văn đang làm nhiệm vụ gì.

- describing a problem?
- comparing two things?
- giving a solution?
- giving a historical background?
- explaining a cause?

#### Sentence completion

Before looking for the answer, predict grammar:

- need a noun?
- need a number?
- need a verb?
- need an adjective?

#### Multiple choice

Cover the options first. Read the question. Find evidence. Then compare options.

**Lưu ý:** Lỗi Reading thường xuất hiện khi người học chọn phương án nghe quen, trong khi đáp án đúng phải dựa trên bằng chứng trong bài đọc.

## 🛠️ Kỹ năng & Khung mẫu — Listening

### 11.1 Bản đồ Listening cho người học sơ cấp

Người học Listening sơ cấp cần đi qua năm tầng xử lý:

1. **Âm:** nghe ra âm thanh trước khi cố dịch nghĩa.
2. **Mặt chữ:** nối âm vừa nghe với dạng từ đã biết.
3. **Cụm:** nhận ra cụm từ, không chỉ từng từ rời.
4. **Ý:** hiểu thông tin chính trong ngữ cảnh.
5. **Câu trả lời:** chuyển thông tin nghe được thành đáp án.

Nhiều bạn bị đứt ở chỗ Sound → Word. Nhìn thì biết comfortable, nghe thì không nhận ra. Nhìn thì biết worked, nghe không thấy /t/. Vì vậy, người học Listening sơ cấp cần transcript, đọc to, dictation ngắn và shadowing.

### 11.2 Listening Micro Skills

#### Skill 1 — Names and spelling

Practise spelling aloud:

- Wilson: W-I-L-S-O-N
- Lan: L-A-N
- Station Road: S-T-A-T-I-O-N Road
- Nguyen: N-G-U-Y-E-N
- Green: G-R-E-E-N

#### Skill 2 — Numbers

Common traps:

- seventeen vs seventy
- thirteen vs thirty
- eighteen vs eighty
- fourteen vs forty
- nineteen vs ninety
- fifteen vs fifty
- sixteen vs sixty

**Mini drill:** read pairs aloud and record: 13/30, 14/40, 15/50, 16/60, 17/70, 18/80, 19/90.

#### Skill 3 — Time

- half past seven = 7:30
- a quarter past nine = 9:15
- a quarter to eight = 7:45
- ten to six = 5:50

#### Skill 4 — Prediction

Before listening, look at the blank:

- Name: likely spelling.
- Date: number + month.
- Price: money.
- Place: location.
- Reason: because/so/due to.

#### Skill 5 — Paraphrase lite

- Audio says: The class is not expensive. Question says: The course is affordable.
- Audio says: She finds it hard to focus. Question says: She has difficulty concentrating.

### 11.3 Bài luyện nghe theo transcript

Có thể dùng các đoạn này làm script giáo viên đọc, audio tự thu hoặc đầu vào text-to-speech. Không cần làm phức tạp. Người học nghe hai lần, trả lời câu hỏi, rồi đọc to transcript.

#### Script 1 — Study Club

**Transcript**

Hi, my name is Anna. I joined an English study club last month. We meet every Wednesday at five thirty in the school library. Each meeting lasts about one hour. We usually practise speaking first, then we review useful vocabulary. I like the club because it is friendly and not too stressful.

**Questions**

1. When did Anna join the club?
2. When do they meet?
3. Where do they meet?
4. How long does each meeting last?
5. Why does Anna like the club?

**Answer key**

1. Last month.
2. Every Wednesday at 5:30.
3. In the school library.
4. About one hour.
5. Because it is friendly and not too stressful.

#### Script 2 — Phone Problem

**Transcript**

Tom wants to study English every night, but he often gets distracted by his phone. He opens a dictionary app, but then he checks messages and watches short videos. His teacher suggests a simple rule: put the phone on airplane mode for twenty minutes and use a notebook for new words.

**Questions**

1. What does Tom want to do every night?
2. What distracts him?
3. What app does he open first?
4. What does his teacher suggest?
5. How long should he put the phone on airplane mode?

**Answer key**

1. Study English.
2. His phone/messages/videos.
3. A dictionary app.
4. Put the phone on airplane mode and use a notebook.
5. Twenty minutes.

#### Script 3 — Healthy Habit

**Transcript**

Mina used to sleep very late before exams. She thought studying until midnight was helpful, but she often felt tired the next morning. This year, she changed her habit. She studies for shorter periods, takes small breaks, and goes to bed before eleven. She says she remembers vocabulary better now.

**Questions**

1. What did Mina use to do before exams?
2. How did she feel the next morning?
3. What did she change this year?
4. What time does she go to bed before?
5. What does she remember better now?

**Answer key**

1. Sleep/study very late.
2. Tired.
3. She studies for shorter periods, takes breaks, and sleeps earlier.
4. Before eleven.
5. Vocabulary.

## 📝 Kịch bản bài học (Lesson Scripts)

### Bài học 7.1 — Relative Clauses

- **Mở bài:** Hôm nay, lớp tập trung làm rõ một điểm nhỏ trong tiếng Anh và dùng ngay điểm đó trong câu thật.
- **Trọng tâm:** relative clauses. Người học cần hiểu điểm này, dùng ngay trong câu và ghi lại để ôn.
- **Mẫu:** Giáo viên viết 3 ví dụ ngắn lên bảng hoặc bài đăng. Người học đọc to, nhận ra mẫu câu hữu ích rồi thay một từ để câu đúng với bản thân.
- **Luyện có hướng dẫn:** Người học hoàn thành 5 câu có kiểm soát. Giáo viên chỉ sửa đúng trọng tâm của buổi học để người học không bị quá tải.
- **Sản phẩm cuối buổi:** Người học dùng trọng tâm của bài để tạo một sản phẩm ngắn: bản ghi âm 30 giây, đoạn 5 câu hoặc bình luận dưới bài đăng.
- **Sửa lỗi:** Chọn một ví dụ ẩn danh, cho thấy câu trước và sau khi sửa, giải thích bằng tiếng Việt rồi yêu cầu cả lớp viết một câu mới theo mẫu đã sửa.
- **Kết buổi:** Khi một điểm nhỏ rõ hơn, nền tảng của người học chắc thêm một phần.
